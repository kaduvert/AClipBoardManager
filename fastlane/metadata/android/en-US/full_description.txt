ClipVault is a deliberately small clipboard manager: one screen with a search
bar and your copied entries, and one settings screen. Nothing else.

Tap any past entry to put it back on the clipboard - it's marked as the
active entry, and selecting it never creates a duplicate or reorders your
history.

Capture works without any of the usual clipboard-reading permissions:

* LSPosed (recommended) - a module hook installed into system_server reads
  clipboard writes system-wide, before Android's background-read
  restrictions even apply.
* Root fallback - an included Magisk module promotes the app to a
  privileged system app so a background service can read clipboard changes
  the way Android's own privileged clipboard tools do.

Settings are limited to what a clipboard history actually needs:

* Private mode, to pause saving entirely
* A configurable history limit (or no limit)
* Clear history

ClipVault makes no network requests, contains no analytics or ads, and
stores everything locally on the device.

Requires either an active LSPosed module or root access to capture
anything - see the project README for setup instructions.
